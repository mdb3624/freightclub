## CHG-855: BOL Source-of-Record Is Single-Party (Shipper-Only), Missing Carrier Attestation

**Original Story:** US-302 (Platform-Generated BOL) — also touches US-303 (BOL/POD Photo Upload & Viewing)
**Discovered By:** BA (via `/roast` council review), 2026-07-14
**Root Cause:** US-302's locked business rule states "Generated BOL becomes the 'source of truth' unless shipper uploads a custom BOL photo" — i.e. the shipper's load-posting data alone produces the canonical BOL, with no in-app OO/carrier attestation step at physical pickup. A five-persona review (Contrarian, Logician, Researcher, Expansionist, Buyer) independently converged on the same finding: a BOL is legally a *receipt*, and its evidentiary value comes from the carrier's attestation of condition/count/time at the actual pickup moment, not from the shipper's pre-populated data. Per 49 CFR / common carrier law and confirmed by how existing platforms handle it (Uber Freight, C.H. Robinson eBOL, the NMFTA Digital LTL Council eBOL standard), liability transfers from shipper to carrier at the moment the carrier signs — a document the platform declares canonical *before* that signature is not a valid BOL, just a shipping label with a BOL-shaped PDF.

**Technical Blocker:** US-302 is COMPLETED and shipped as shipper-only generation (`BillingService.generateBol`, GET `/api/v1/loads/{id}/bol`, no carrier confirmation step). Reworking it in place would violate the Sequential Lock/CHG protocol — this must go through a versioned follow-up story, not a silent edit to a COMPLETED story.

**Options:**
1. **Add a carrier confirm-and-lock step (recommended):** Keep US-302's shipper-side draft generation as-is (pre-populates commodity/weight/origin-destination/carrier assignment). Add a new OO-side "confirm pickup" action — ideally tied to the existing claim/pickup event already in the load lifecycle — that captures a timestamp, the OO's attestation, and an optional exception/photo note (reusing US-303's photo upload infra), then locks the BOL as canonical and immutable. Shipper-only-generated BOLs remain valid as pre-pickup drafts, never as the final record.
2. **Do nothing; accept current shipper-only model as final.** Keeps FreightClub's BOL as effectively a shipping label, not a legally robust contract of carriage — leaves the liability gap the council flagged (platform effectively asserting facts about freight it never witnessed) and the dispute-resolution gap (no captured carrier attestation at pickup) unaddressed.
3. **Full redesign as platform-neutral escrow:** Store shipper-entered and carrier-entered data as two separate immutable, timestamped fields on the same record (per the Buyer persona's ask), rather than one party's action producing a single "canonical" document. Higher build cost than Option 1, but removes any perception of platform bias toward either party.

**Recommendation:** Option 1 — matches real-world BOL practice and existing industry platforms exactly (shipper drafts, carrier confirms/signs at pickup), reuses the load-claim/pickup event and US-303 photo-upload infra already in place, and is the cheapest path that actually fixes the legal/evidentiary gap rather than papering over it.

**Next Steps:**
1. BA elaborates AC for a new story (candidate ID: **US-302-v2**, or next available in the 3xx/7xx range per Story_Map numbering) covering: OO-side pickup-confirmation UI action, timestamp + optional exception/photo capture, BOL immutability after lock.
2. ARCHITECT confirms whether the existing load-claim/pickup domain event can carry the confirmation payload, or whether a new event/state is needed.
3. HFD designs the OO-side confirm-and-lock interaction per Carrier Design System (mobile-first, glove-friendly, tap-only).
4. CODER implements per Sequential Lock Protocol once BA/ARCH/HFD inputs are locked.
